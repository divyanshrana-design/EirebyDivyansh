Research & statistics

The numbers we quote across the site come from these primary studies and industry reports.

  • Asana — Anatomy of Work Index
    Asana 2023

    Source for the often quoted "13.7 hours a week lost to scattered tools, status meetings and context switching" stat (≈89 working days a year).

    asana.com/resources/anatomy-of-work
  • PMI — Pulse of the Profession 2023
    Project Management Institute 2023

    Hybrid project management adoption up 57.5% since 2020. Annual industry survey of how organisations actually deliver projects.

    pmi.org/learning/library/pulse-of-the-profession-2023
  • Fortune Business Insights — Project Management Software Market
    Fortune Business Insights 2024

    Source for the "$15bn global PM software market in 2025" figure. Includes growth forecasts to 2030.

    fortunebusinessinsights.com/project-management-software-market-103284
  • Wellingtone — State of Project Management 2024
    Wellingtone 2024

    Source for "90% of a PM's time is spent communicating" and other PM benchmarking data, with a strong UK and Ireland sample base.

    wellingtone.co.uk/state-of-project-management-2024
  • McKinsey — The State of Organizations 2023
    McKinsey & Company 2023

    Background data on hybrid working and team productivity used in our charts.

    mckinsey.com/...the-state-of-organizations-2023

Methodologies

Foundational sources for the Agile, Scrum, Kanban, Waterfall and Hybrid descriptions on the Methodologies page.

  • The Agile Manifesto
    2001

    The original 4 values and 12 principles that started the Agile movement. Still the cleanest explanation of what Agile actually is.

    agilemanifesto.org
  • The Scrum Guide
    Schwaber & Sutherland 2020

    The definitive 13 page Scrum reference. Free to download. Worth reading in full at least once.

    scrumguides.org/scrum-guide.html
  • Atlassian — Kanban Guide
    Atlassian

    Approachable, well illustrated guide to Kanban for small teams, including WIP limits and flow metrics.

    atlassian.com/agile/kanban
  • PMI — Disciplined Agile Delivery (Hybrid framework)
    Project Management Institute

    PMI's official take on hybrid PM. Heavyweight, but useful for understanding why Hybrid is the dominant approach.

    pmi.org/disciplined-agile
  • PRINCE2 — Methodology overview
    AXELOS

    The structured Waterfall style methodology widely used by Irish public sector and EU funded projects.

    prince2.com/uk/what-is-prince2

AI for business

The reports, prompt libraries and tool docs behind the AI for Your Business chapter.

  • McKinsey — The State of AI 2024
    McKinsey & Company 2024

    Annual benchmark of AI adoption in business, with breakdown by company size and function. Source for several of our productivity figures.

    mckinsey.com/...the-state-of-ai
  • Harvard Business Review — How Generative AI Can Augment Human Creativity
    HBR 2023

    Background reading on how small teams can use AI as a thinking partner, not just a content factory.

    hbr.org/...generative-ai-can-augment-human-creativity
  • OpenAI — Prompt Engineering Guide
    OpenAI

    Official guidance on writing better prompts. The structure behind our 30 prompt library.

    platform.openai.com/docs/guides/prompt-engineering
  • Anthropic — Prompting tips for Claude
    Anthropic

    Best practice prompts for Claude, the model that powers many of our recommended workflows.

    anthropic.com/news/prompting-long-context
  • Data Protection Commission Ireland — AI & Data Protection
    DPC Ireland

    The Irish regulator's official guidance on using AI responsibly. Required reading for any SME piping customer data through an LLM.

    dataprotection.ie/...artificial-intelligence-and-data-protection

PM tools & software

Official product pages for the PM tools we recommend on the Tools & Resources page.

  • Trello

    Free, simple Kanban board. Best starting point for solo operators and small charities.

    trello.com
  • Asana

    Heavier than Trello, with timelines, forms and a real reporting layer.

    asana.com
  • Notion

    Docs, databases and a wiki, all in one. Strong AI features now built in.

    notion.so
  • Jira

    Heavyweight, for software teams that want full Scrum support.

    atlassian.com/software/jira
  • Monday.com

    Highly visual work OS, popular with marketing and operations teams.

    monday.com
  • ClickUp

    "All in one" tool that tries to be Asana, Notion and Trello at once. Powerful, but a learning curve.

    clickup.com

Irish SME resources

Official Irish supports, regulators and grant bodies referenced throughout the site.

  • Local Enterprise Office (LEO)

    Your first stop for grants, mentoring, training and Trading Online Voucher support if you're a small Irish business.

    localenterprise.ie
  • Charities Regulator (Ireland)

    Statutory body for Irish charities. Reporting requirements, governance code and the official charity register.

    charitiesregulator.ie
  • Data Protection Commission

    Irish GDPR regulator. Excellent SME and charity specific guidance on data protection.

    dataprotection.ie
  • Enterprise Ireland

    For scaling Irish companies — funding, R&D supports and export development.

    enterprise-ireland.com
  • Revenue — Charities and Sports Bodies

    Official tax info for Irish charities, sports clubs and not for profits.

    revenue.ie/...charities-and-sports-bodies

Books & long reads

If you want to go deeper than this guide, start here.

  • The Mythical Man Month — Frederick Brooks

    A 1975 classic that explains why adding people to a late project makes it later. Still the single most useful PM book ever written.

    goodreads.com/...The_Mythical_Man_Month
  • The Phoenix Project — Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, George Spafford

    A novel about an IT manager rescuing a failing project. Reads like a thriller, teaches DevOps and lean PM by stealth.

    goodreads.com/...the-phoenix-project
  • Making Things Happen — Scott Berkun

    Practical, opinionated PM advice from an ex Microsoft project lead. Excellent on the people side of the job.

    goodreads.com/...Making_Things_Happen
  • Shape Up — Ryan Singer / Basecamp

    Free online book describing 37signals' alternative to Scrum: 6 week cycles with a 2 week cool down. Great for small product teams.

    basecamp.com/shapeup
